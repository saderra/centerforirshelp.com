What to Expect

  • Depending on how much you owe, IRS will require a detailed financial disclosure to determine your ability to pay.
  • IRS may summons bank account and other records from third parties.
  • IRS may summons you to testify and/or produce records.
  • IRS will seek to get you into compliance with filing.

Things To Know

  • You must get into current compliance by filing all unfiled personal and business tax returns before IRS will work with you to discuss Collection Alternatives to its Enforced Collection Actions.
  • IRS will assume the amount you owe is correct (including penalties and interest).
  • Unpaid tax debts referred for criminal investigation and prosecution of Federal crimes are on the rise.
  • "Pennies on the dollar" is not a real thing.
  • If your balance is over $50,000, IRS will generally file a Notice of Federal Tax Lien; but, will not if yourbalance is under $50,000.
  • Depending on how much you owe, IRS will require a detailed financial disclosure to determine your ability to pay.

Get Prepared: The Do's

  • IRS records are not always correct. Get Account Transcripts from IRS and review them against your records.
